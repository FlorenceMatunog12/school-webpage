Timeline of Human Civilization

3100 BCE – Ancient Egypt

The unification of Upper and Lower Egypt under Pharaoh Narmer marks the start of one of the world's longest-lasting civilizations.

2600 BCE – Indus Valley Civilization

Advanced city planning, sewer systems, and trade artifacts like the Dancing Girl statue reflect a rich culture.

500 BCE – Classical Greece

Philosophy, democracy, and the arts flourish with figures like Socrates, Plato, and Aristotle.

800 CE – Islamic Golden Age

Scientific discovery, algebra, medical innovation, and translations of Greek texts thrive in Baghdad and Cordoba.

1500 CE – Renaissance

Revival of classical art, literature, and learning. Key figures: Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, and Galileo.

21st Century – Digital Preservation

Global efforts to preserve heritage digitally through 3D scans, virtual museums, and cloud archives.
