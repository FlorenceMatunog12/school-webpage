pyramid

A "pyramid story" can refer to two distinct concepts: a storytelling structure and the historical pyramids of Egypt. The first, Freytag's Pyramid, is a narrative structure that divides a story into five stages: exposition, rising action, climax, falling action, and resolution.

he Mahabharata is a vast Indian epic poem centered on a dynastic struggle for the throne of Hastinapura between two families of cousins, the Pandavas and the Kauravas.

Camiguin

Camiguin Island, known as the "Island Born of Fire," has a rich history marked by volcanic activity, Spanish colonization, and indigenous settlement. It was initially inhabited by the Manobo people, later visited by explorers like Magellan and Legazpi, and saw the establishment of Spanish settlements, including the first in Guinsiliban. Volcanic eruptions, particularly in the 1870s, significantly shaped its landscape and history.

Catarman Old Church Ruins

The Old Church Ruins in Catarman, Camiguin, also known as Guiob Church Ruins, are a historical landmark that tells a story of resilience and the power of nature. Originally a Spanish-era church built in the 16th century, it was destroyed by the eruption of Mt. Vulcan in 1871, along with much of the old town of Catarman.

The Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications built over centuries, primarily to protect against nomadic groups from the north.

Moai

The moai are massive, monolithic statues located on Rapa Nui (Easter Island) and are a testament to the ingenuity and cultural richness of the Polynesian people who created them. These statues, primarily carved from solidified volcanic ash, represent deified ancestors and were erected on stone platforms called ahu.
